Character Arc

Irving Bailiff is the most senior member of the Macrodata Refinement team and initially presents as a devoted Lumon loyalist. He reveres Kier Eagan, the company's founder, and eagerly recites company handbook passages. His devotion to Lumon's corporate culture is genuine and deeply felt, making him an unexpected source of both humor and pathos.

Irving's carefully maintained composure begins to crack when he develops a tender romance with Burt Goodman from the Optics and Design department ‚Äî a relationship that Lumon has gone to great lengths to prevent by fostering manufactured rivalry between their departments. Their connection is one of the show's most moving storylines, a love that persists despite institutional barriers designed to make it impossible.

As Irving discovers more about Lumon's deceptions, his loyalty transforms into righteous fury. His outie, meanwhile, is revealed to be a haunted man who paints the same dark corridor over and over and has been researching severed employees. The Season 1 finale shows outie Irving driving to Burt's house, suggesting a connection between their selves that transcends the severance procedure. In Season 2, Irving's dual journey deepens as both his innie and outie pursue the truth about Lumon through very different means.

John Turturro plays Irving Bailiff in Severance. Turturro, an acclaimed character actor known for films by the Coen Brothers and Spike Lee, brings warmth and gravitas to the role.

Outside of work, Irving's outie obsessively paints dark images of a long black corridor that appears to be the entrance to the testing floor at Lumon. This suggests that memories or impressions from his innie life are bleeding through the severance procedure.

Irving and Burt develop a romantic connection on the severed floor despite Lumon fostering animosity between their departments. Their relationship is one of the show's most beloved storylines, with fans rooting for them across both seasons.

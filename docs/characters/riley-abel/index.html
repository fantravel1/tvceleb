Character Arc

Riley Abel is Ellie's best friend and first love, a bold, charismatic teenage girl who leaves the FEDRA military school in Boston to join the Fireflies, the rebel group fighting against the authoritarian quarantine zone government. Her departure devastates Ellie, who felt abandoned by the one person who made the oppressive boarding school life bearable.

Riley returns to find Ellie one night, sneaking her out of the dormitory for an adventure in an abandoned shopping mall. Their night together ‚Äî exploring a Halloween store, playing arcade games, riding a carousel, and sharing a first kiss on a photo booth strip ‚Äî is one of the most tender and achingly real depictions of teenage love ever put on screen. It is a night of pure joy in a world that rarely allows any.

The tragedy strikes when they are attacked by an infected hiding in the mall. Both girls are bitten. Riley, knowing what comes next, urges Ellie that they have two options: take the easy way out, or "be all poetic and just lose our minds together." They choose to wait together, but only one of them turns. Riley succumbs to the infection, and Ellie discovers she is immune ‚Äî a revelation born from the worst night of her life.

Riley's death is the foundational trauma that shapes everything about Ellie. Her survivor's guilt, her willingness to die for a cure, her difficulty trusting people, and her ferocious determination to make her life mean something ‚Äî all of it traces back to the night she lost Riley. Though she appears in only one episode, Riley is the ghost that haunts the entire series.

Storm Reid plays Riley Abel in The Last of Us. Reid is an American actress known for her roles in Euphoria, A Wrinkle in Time, and The Invisible Man. Her chemistry with Bella Ramsey was widely praised by critics and audiences.

Yes, Riley Abel appears in The Last of Us: Left Behind, a DLC expansion for the original game. The HBO show's Episode 7 ("Left Behind") closely adapts this storyline, expanding the mall sequence and deepening the romantic relationship between Ellie and Riley.

Ellie was bitten alongside Riley in the abandoned mall. Riley turned into an infected and died, but Ellie never turned. The Season 1 finale suggests Ellie's immunity may be connected to her mother Anna being bitten during labor, with the Cordyceps crossing to Ellie in utero.

Riley's death is the defining trauma of Ellie's life. Surviving when Riley did not created profound survivor's guilt that drives Ellie's willingness to sacrifice herself for a vaccine. Riley was also Ellie's first love, and losing her shapes Ellie's difficulty forming attachments and her fierce protectiveness of the people she does let in.

Character Arc

Ada Shelby is the only sister among the Shelby siblings and arguably the most independent-minded of them all. From the very beginning, Ada resists the gravitational pull of the family business, pursuing her own path through a secret romance with communist organizer Freddie Thorne ‚Äî a relationship that puts her at odds with Tommy and the rest of the Blinders.

Ada's political awakening through Freddie and her involvement in socialist and communist movements gives her a perspective that none of her brothers possess. She sees the world beyond the narrow confines of Small Heath and understands that the Shelby empire, for all its power, is built on exploitation. This ideological tension creates some of the show's most compelling family conflicts.

After Freddie's death, Ada moves to London and builds an independent life, raising her son Karl while establishing herself as a woman of substance in her own right. Yet the Shelby blood runs deep, and she is repeatedly drawn back into family affairs ‚Äî not as a pawn, but as a power player. By the later seasons, Ada emerges as a key strategic figure, using her intelligence and connections to protect the family from both external threats and internal collapse.

In the final season, with Polly gone, Ada steps into the role of family matriarch and proves herself every bit as formidable. Her evolution from rebellious sister to pillar of the Shelby dynasty mirrors the show's larger themes of how power and family loyalty can reshape even the most resistant individuals.

Trivia & Fun Facts

  • Sophie Rundle portrayed Ada's evolution from a rebellious young woman to a politically savvy businesswoman over six seasons.
  • Ada's romance with a communist was based on real tensions between criminal gangs and political movements in 1920s Birmingham.
  • Sophie Rundle has said Ada is the Shelby most likely to have escaped the family business entirely.

? Frequently Asked Questions

Sophie Rundle plays Ada Shelby in Peaky Blinders. Rundle has also appeared in Gentleman Jack, Bodyguard, and The Nest.

Ada's first husband is Freddie Thorne, a communist organizer and war veteran who dies of pestilence between Seasons 1 and 2. She later has a relationship with Colonel Ben Younger in later seasons.

Ada initially distances herself from the family business but gradually becomes more involved. By the final seasons, she serves as a key strategic advisor and, after Polly's death, steps into the role of family matriarch.

Ada Shelby is a fictional character. While the Peaky Blinders gang was real, the specific Shelby family members are fictional creations by showrunner Steven Knight.

Biography

Jenna Marie Ortega is an American actress born on September 27, 2002, in Coachella Valley, California. She is of Mexican and Puerto Rican descent and began acting at a young age, landing her first significant role as Young Jane in the CW series Jane the Virgin (2014-2019). She quickly became one of the most in-demand young actresses in Hollywood.

Ortega rose to superstardom as the title character in Netflix's Wednesday (2022-present), a coming-of-age series centered on Wednesday Addams at Nevermore Academy. Her deadpan, darkly comedic performance captured audiences worldwide, and the show became one of Netflix's most-watched series ever. She also choreographed Wednesday's viral dance scene, which became a global social media phenomenon. The role earned her a Golden Globe nomination and established her as a generational star.

Beyond Wednesday, Ortega has become a prominent figure in the horror genre, starring in Scream (2022) and Scream VI (2023) as Tara Carpenter, and X (2022) directed by Ti West. She also appeared in the Disney Channel series Stuck in the Middle (2016-2018), the Netflix film Yes Day (2021), and the animated film Wendell & Wild (2022). Ortega has been praised for her work ethic, screen presence, and ability to balance mainstream appeal with genre credibility.

Biography

John Burke Krasinski is an American actor, filmmaker, and screenwriter born on October 20, 1979, in Newton, Massachusetts. He studied playwriting at Brown University and later trained at the National Theater Institute and the Royal Shakespeare Company. He began his career with small television and film roles before landing the part that would define his career.

Krasinski became beloved worldwide as Jim Halpert, the charming, everyman sales representative at Dunder Mifflin, in NBC's The Office (2005-2013). His naturalistic performance, particularly his trademark looks to camera and his romance with Pam Beesly (Jenna Fischer), became central to the show's appeal. He appeared in all nine seasons and also served as a producer and occasional director on the series.

Beyond The Office, Krasinski has established himself as a major filmmaker. He wrote, directed, and starred in the horror film A Quiet Place (2018), which became a critical and commercial blockbuster, followed by A Quiet Place Part II (2020). He also created and hosted the web series Some Good News during the COVID-19 pandemic, which was later acquired by ViacomCBS. In 2021, he joined the Marvel Cinematic Universe as Reed Richards / Mister Fantastic in Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness. He also stars as the title character in the Amazon Prime Video series Jack Ryan (2018-2023).

Biography

Lee Byung-hun is a South Korean actor born on July 12, 1970, in Seoul, South Korea. He studied at Hanyang University and began his acting career in the early 1990s. He became one of South Korea's biggest stars, known as one of the "Four Princes" of Korean entertainment, and has successfully crossed over into Hollywood blockbusters.

Lee plays the enigmatic Front Man, the masked overseer of the deadly games, in the Netflix series Squid Game (2021-present). His commanding, mysterious presence in the role added an extra layer of intrigue to the series, and the revelation of his character's identity became one of the show's most talked-about moments. His involvement brought additional star power to an already stacked cast.

Beyond Squid Game, Lee has had an extraordinary dual career in Korean and Hollywood cinema. His Korean credits include the classic films Joint Security Area (2000), A Bittersweet Life (2005), and I Saw the Devil (2010). In Hollywood, he appeared in G.I. Joe: The Rise of Cobra (2009) and its sequel, RED 2 (2013), Terminator Genisys (2015), and The Magnificent Seven (2016). He won the Grand Bell Award multiple times and is considered one of the most internationally successful Korean actors ever.

Biography

Benjamin Joseph Manaly Novak, known as B.J. Novak, is an American actor, writer, comedian, author, and director born on July 31, 1979, in Newton, Massachusetts. He attended Newton South High School alongside future co-star John Krasinski and graduated from Harvard University in 2001, where he was president of the Harvard Lampoon.

Novak played the ambitious, self-absorbed temp-turned-executive Ryan Howard in NBC's The Office (2005-2013). Beyond his acting role, Novak was one of the show's most prolific writers and served as an executive producer, making him one of the key creative forces behind the series. He wrote many of the show's most acclaimed episodes, including "Diversity Day" and "Threat Level Midnight," and directed several episodes as well.

Outside of The Office, Novak has become a multi-hyphenate creative force. He wrote the New York Times bestselling children's book The Book with No Pictures (2014) and the short story collection One More Thing: Stories and Other Stories (2014). He wrote and directed the film Vengeance (2022), and has appeared in films including Inglourious Basterds (2009), Saving Mr. Banks (2013), and The Founder (2016). He also co-created the FX anthology series The Premise (2021).

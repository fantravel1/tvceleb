Biography

Nicola Mary Coughlan is an Irish actress born on January 9, 1987, in Galway, Ireland. She studied at the Oxford School of Drama and Birmingham School of Acting. She began her career in Irish and British theater before breaking through on television with two major roles that showcased her comedic and dramatic range.

Coughlan gained her first wave of recognition as Clare Devlin in the Channel 4/Netflix comedy Derry Girls (2018-2022), a beloved sitcom set during the Troubles in Northern Ireland. She simultaneously rose to global fame as Penelope Featherington in Netflix's Bridgerton (2020-present). Her portrayal of the shy wallflower who secretly authors the scandalous Lady Whistledown gossip column became a fan favorite. In Season 3, Penelope's love story with Colin Bridgerton took center stage, making Coughlan the romantic lead.

Coughlan has become a prominent figure in discussions about body positivity and representation in media, speaking openly about the pressures faced by women in the entertainment industry. She has used her platform to challenge beauty standards and advocate for more inclusive casting. Beyond Bridgerton and Derry Girls, she has appeared in the film Big Mood (2024) and continues to take on roles that showcase her range.

Biography

Melanie Jayne Lynskey is a New Zealand actress born on May 16, 1977, in New Plymouth, New Zealand. She made a remarkable film debut at age sixteen as Pauline Parker in Peter Jackson's Heavenly Creatures (1994), a performance that earned her immediate international attention and a New Zealand Film Award for Best Actress.

Lynskey has had a long, acclaimed career as a character actress before reaching her widest audience as Kathleen Coghlan, the ruthless revolutionary leader in the HBO series The Last of Us (2023-present). Her portrayal of the grief-stricken, vengeful leader of a Kansas City resistance group showcased her ability to portray complex, morally ambiguous characters. The role introduced her to a massive new audience.

Beyond The Last of Us, Lynskey earned critical acclaim and a Critics' Choice Award for her lead role in the Showtime series Yellowjackets (2021-present), playing the adult version of Shauna. She has had memorable roles in numerous films including But I'm a Cheerleader (1999), Sweet Home Alabama (2002), Away We Go (2009), Up in the Air (2009), and Don't Look Up (2021). She was also a series regular on Two and a Half Men (2004-2015) as Rose. Lynskey is widely regarded as one of the most talented character actresses of her generation.

Biography

Golda Rosheuvel is a British-Guyanese actress born on June 1, 1970, in Guyana. She moved to the United Kingdom as a child and trained at the Bristol Old Vic Theatre School. She built an extensive career in British theater spanning decades, performing with the Royal Shakespeare Company, the National Theatre, and in numerous West End productions.

Rosheuvel gained international acclaim as Queen Charlotte, the regal, commanding, and scene-stealing monarch, in Netflix's Bridgerton (2020-present). Her powerful portrayal of the queen who oversees the marriage market of Regency-era London with equal parts authority and vulnerability became one of the show's most beloved elements. The character proved so popular that she headlined the spin-off series Queen Charlotte: A Bridgerton Story (2023).

Before Bridgerton, Rosheuvel had an extensive career in British theater and television, appearing in productions ranging from Shakespeare to contemporary drama. Her television credits include Luther (2019), Dune (2020), and the soap opera Coronation Street. She is an advocate for diversity in the performing arts and has spoken about the significance of color-conscious casting in period dramas. Her portrayal of Queen Charlotte helped redefine how audiences think about historical representation on screen.

Biography

Steven John Carell is an American actor, comedian, producer, writer, and director born on August 16, 1962, in Concord, Massachusetts. He studied history at Denison University and later trained at the Second City comedy troupe in Chicago, where he honed his improvisational skills alongside Stephen Colbert and other future comedy stars.

Carell became a household name as Michael Scott, the lovably incompetent regional manager of the Scranton branch of Dunder Mifflin Paper Company, in NBC's The Office (2005-2013). His performance transformed what was initially a low-rated adaptation of the British series into one of the most beloved comedies in television history. He played the role for seven seasons and received a Golden Globe Award for Best Actor in a Television Series - Musical or Comedy, along with six Emmy nominations.

Beyond The Office, Carell has built one of the most versatile careers in Hollywood. His comedic hits include The 40-Year-Old Virgin (2005), Anchorman (2004), and the Despicable Me franchise (2010-2024, as the voice of Gru). He also earned an Academy Award nomination for Best Actor for his dramatic turn in Foxcatcher (2014) and has delivered acclaimed performances in The Big Short (2015), Beautiful Boy (2018), and The Morning Show (2019-present) on Apple TV+.

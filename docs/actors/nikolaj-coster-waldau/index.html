Biography

Nikolaj Coster-Waldau is a Danish actor born on July 27, 1970, in Rudk√∏bing, Denmark. He graduated from the Danish National School of Performing Arts in Copenhagen in 1993 and quickly became one of Denmark's most prominent actors, starring in the film Nightwatch (1994) and its American remake.

Coster-Waldau achieved international fame as Jaime Lannister, the morally complex "Kingslayer," in HBO's Game of Thrones (2011-2019). His portrayal of the conflicted knight who evolves from a seemingly villainous figure to a more sympathetic character on a path of redemption became one of the show's most nuanced character arcs. He received two Primetime Emmy nominations for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Drama Series for the role.

Outside of Game of Thrones, Coster-Waldau has appeared in Hollywood productions including Black Hawk Down (2001), Oblivion (2013), Gods of Egypt (2016), and Shot Caller (2017). He has remained active in Scandinavian cinema and has served as a UNDP Goodwill Ambassador since 2016, focusing on climate change and sustainable development goals. He continues to balance international and Danish film projects.

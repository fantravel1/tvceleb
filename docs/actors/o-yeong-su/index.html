Biography

O Yeong-su is a South Korean actor born on October 19, 1944, in Kaepung County, in what is now North Korea. He fled south during the Korean War and grew up in South Korea. He trained as a stage actor and spent decades as one of the most respected theater performers in South Korea, known for his classical work and stage presence.

O gained worldwide recognition as Oh Il-nam (Player 001), the elderly participant in the deadly games, in the Netflix series Squid Game (2021-present). His gentle, mysterious performance as the seemingly frail old man who joins the games concealed one of the series' most shocking plot twists. The role earned him a Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor in a Television Series, making him the first Korean actor to win a Golden Globe.

Before Squid Game, O had an illustrious career spanning over five decades, primarily in Korean theater. He is considered a legend of Korean stage acting and has performed in countless productions. His film work includes Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter... and Spring (2003), directed by Kim Ki-duk. Despite his long career, it was Squid Game that introduced him to audiences around the world at the age of 77.

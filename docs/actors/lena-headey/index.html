Biography

Lena Kathren Headey is a British actress born on October 3, 1973, in Hamilton, Bermuda, and raised in England. She trained at the Shelley Park Theatre and began her film career in the early 1990s, appearing in films such as The Remains of the Day (1993) and The Jungle Book (1994).

Headey achieved her greatest fame as Cersei Lannister in HBO's Game of Thrones (2011-2019). Her portrayal of the cunning, ruthless, and deeply complex Queen of the Seven Kingdoms is widely regarded as one of the finest villain performances in television history. The role earned her five Primetime Emmy nominations for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series and showcased her ability to convey power, vulnerability, and menace simultaneously.

Beyond Game of Thrones, Headey voiced Commander Shepard in the video game Mass Effect series, played Queen Gorgo in 300 (2006) and its sequel, and starred as Sarah Connor in the Fox series Terminator: The Sarah Connor Chronicles (2008-2009). She also appeared in the films Dredd (2012), Pride and Prejudice and Zombies (2016), and Fighting with My Family (2019). Headey is highly respected for her intense screen presence and commitment to complex female characters.

Biography

Jonathan Ray Banks is an American actor born on January 31, 1947, in Washington, D.C. He was raised in Indiana and studied at Indiana University before beginning his acting career in the 1970s. Banks spent decades as a working character actor in Hollywood, appearing in films and television shows across multiple genres.

Banks is most widely recognized for his role as Mike Ehrmantraut, the stoic and resourceful former police officer turned fixer, in both Breaking Bad (2009-2013) and Better Call Saul (2015-2022). His understated yet powerful performance as Mike became a fan favorite, earning him five Primetime Emmy Award nominations for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Drama Series. The character's quiet intensity and moral complexity made Mike one of the most memorable figures in the Breaking Bad universe.

Prior to Breaking Bad, Banks had a long career in film and television, with notable roles including the villain in Beverly Hills Cop (1984) and Frank McPike in the CBS series Wiseguy (1987-1990). He also appeared in Mudbound (2017), Horrible Bosses (2011), and the comedy series Community. Banks is widely regarded as one of the finest character actors of his generation.

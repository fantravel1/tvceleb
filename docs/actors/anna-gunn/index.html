Biography

Anna Gunn is an American actress born on August 11, 1968, in Cleveland, Ohio. She grew up in Santa Fe, New Mexico, and studied acting at Northwestern University. Gunn began her career in theater and took on early television roles in shows like Seinfeld, NYPD Blue, and Deadwood before her career-defining role.

Gunn is best known for her portrayal of Skyler White, the wife of Walter White, in the AMC drama Breaking Bad (2008-2013). Her nuanced performance as a woman struggling with her husband's criminal double life earned her two consecutive Primetime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series in 2013 and 2014. The role sparked significant cultural conversation about how audiences perceive female characters in television.

Outside of Breaking Bad, Gunn has appeared in the Shonda Rhimes legal drama Gracepoint (2014), the British series Shetland, and films such as Equity (2016), one of the few Wall Street films centered on women. She continues to work in both television and film, bringing depth and intelligence to every role she takes on.

Biography

Sophie Belinda Turner is an English actress born on February 21, 1996, in Northampton, England. She began acting at a young age at the Playbox Theatre Company and was cast in her career-defining role at just thirteen years old, launching one of the most prominent young careers in television.

Turner gained worldwide recognition as Sansa Stark in HBO's Game of Thrones (2011-2019). Over eight seasons, she portrayed Sansa's remarkable transformation from a naive young girl dreaming of a fairy-tale life to a politically savvy and resilient leader who ultimately becomes Queen in the North. Her performance in the later seasons earned particular critical acclaim, and she received an Emmy nomination for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series for the final season.

Beyond Game of Thrones, Turner entered the film world as the young Jean Grey / Phoenix in X-Men: Apocalypse (2016) and Dark Phoenix (2019), taking over the role from Famke Janssen. She also starred in the thriller Survive (2020) for Quibi and the Starz miniseries Joan (2024). Turner has been open about mental health awareness and has used her platform to advocate for support of young people dealing with anxiety and depression.

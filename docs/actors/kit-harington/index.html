Biography

Christopher Catesby Harington, known as Kit Harington, is an English actor born on December 26, 1986, in London, England. He trained at the Royal Central School of Speech and Drama and made his professional acting debut in the West End production of War Horse (2009) before being cast in the role that would make him a global star.

Harington rose to international fame as Jon Snow in the HBO epic fantasy series Game of Thrones (2011-2019). His portrayal of the honorable, brooding bastard son of Ned Stark who rises to become King in the North and plays a pivotal role in the fight against the Night King became one of the most iconic characters in television history. The role earned him two Primetime Emmy nominations for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series.

Beyond Game of Thrones, Harington has appeared in films including Pompeii (2014), Testament of Youth (2014), and voiced Eret in the How to Train Your Dragon franchise. He joined the Marvel Cinematic Universe as Dane Whitman / Black Knight in Eternals (2021). Harington has also returned to the stage, appearing in productions at the London Donmar Warehouse. He is married to his former Game of Thrones co-star Rose Leslie.

Biography

Peter Hayden Dinklage is an American actor born on June 11, 1969, in Morristown, New Jersey. He has achondroplasia, a common form of dwarfism, and has been a powerful advocate for better representation and more complex roles for actors with dwarfism throughout his career. He studied acting at Bennington College in Vermont and spent years performing in theater and independent films before his major breakthrough.

Dinklage achieved worldwide fame for his portrayal of Tyrion Lannister in HBO's Game of Thrones (2011-2019). His witty, complex, and deeply human performance earned him four Primetime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Drama Series, making him the most awarded actor in the show's history and one of the most decorated supporting actors in television. His Emmy wins came in 2011, 2015, 2018, and 2019.

Beyond Game of Thrones, Dinklage has had a distinguished career in film, including memorable roles in The Station Agent (2003), which earned him widespread indie acclaim, Elf (2003), X-Men: Days of Future Past (2014) as Bolivar Trask, Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri (2017), and Cyrano (2021) in the title role. He is widely regarded as one of the finest actors of his generation.

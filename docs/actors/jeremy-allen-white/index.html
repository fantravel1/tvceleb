Biography

Jeremy Allen White is an American actor born on February 17, 1991, in Brooklyn, New York. He trained at the Professional Performing Arts School in Manhattan alongside future stars like Timoth√©e Chalamet. He began his professional career as a teenager and spent nearly a decade building his reputation as a reliable character actor.

White first gained recognition as Philip "Lip" Gallagher, the intellectually gifted eldest Gallagher sibling, in the Showtime series Shameless (2011-2021). However, he achieved his greatest success as Carmen "Carmy" Berzatto, a fine-dining chef who returns to Chicago to run his late brother's struggling Italian beef sandwich shop, in the FX series The Bear (2022-present). His intense, deeply felt performance has earned him consecutive Primetime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Comedy Series and Golden Globe Awards, establishing him as one of the most acclaimed actors of his generation.

Beyond television, White has appeared in independent films including After Everything (2018) and MoviePass, MovieCrash (2022). He is set to star as Bruce Springsteen in the biopic Deliver Me from Nowhere and has become a prominent figure in popular culture, known for his naturalistic acting style and intense commitment to his roles.
